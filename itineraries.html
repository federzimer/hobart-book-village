Plan Your Visit

Whether you have a few hours or a full week, we've built the perfect itinerary to help you make the most of Hobart Book Village and the surrounding Catskills.

1
The Perfect Day in Hobart
A full day of books, food, and village charm
10:00 AM

Arrive & Coffee

Roll into the village and grab a coffee to fuel your bookshop crawl. Stroll Main Street and take in the quiet charm before the shops open.

11:00 AM

Start at Blenheim Hill Books

Warm up with one of the village's most beloved shops. Browse mysteries, poetry, women's studies, and a wonderful children's section.

12:00 PM

Wm. H. Adams' Antiquarian

Step into three floors of rare books, many printed before 1850. History, architecture, art, and literature from centuries past.

1:00 PM

Lunch at The Inn Bar

Take a break with seasonal bites and a craft cocktail or local brew at the Book Village Inn's bar. You've earned it.

2:00 PM

Liberty Rock & Creative Corner

Hit the village's largest bookstore, then pop into Creative Corner for cookbooks, crafts, and locally made goods.

3:30 PM

The Shops Around the Corner

Cross over to 40 Cornell Ave for More Good Books, Quarry Books (honor system!), and the other unique shops in the annex.

4:30 PM

One Last Browse & Sunset

Circle back to whichever shop caught your eye earlier. Grab your finds, then enjoy a golden hour walk through the village before heading home.

💡

Pro tip: Most shops are open Saturday & Sunday, 11–5. Plan your day trip on a weekend for the full experience.

1
Saturday — The Full Village Tour
Settle in and explore every bookshop
Morning

Check In & Coffee

Arrive at the Book Village Inn (or your Airbnb), drop your bags, and stroll Main Street with a coffee in hand.

11 AM

Bookshop Crawl: Part 1

Start with Blenheim Hill Books, Wm. H. Adams' Antiquarian, and Liberty Rock Books. Take your time — you have all weekend.

1 PM

Lunch at The Inn Bar

Seasonal food, craft cocktails, and the warmth of the village's favorite gathering spot.

Afternoon

Bookshop Crawl: Part 2

Creative Corner, NY Books & Ephemera, and the Shops Around the Corner. Find your treasures.

Evening

Dinner & Reading

Dine locally, then curl up at the Inn with your new haul. There's no better way to spend a Saturday night.

2
Sunday — Nature & Slow Morning
Trails, farm stands, and a farewell browse
Morning

Slow Start & Walk

Sleep in, grab breakfast, and take a walk along the West Branch of the Delaware River or explore nearby covered bridges.

Late AM

Catskill Hiking

Drive 20 minutes to trailheads for moderate hikes with stunning mountain views. The Catskills don't disappoint in any season.

Afternoon

Farewell Browse & Farm Stands

Pop back into your favorite shop for that book you almost bought yesterday. Pick up local produce or cheese at a nearby farm stand.

💡

Pro tip: Book the Inn for Saturday night — you'll be steps from every shop and won't have to worry about the drive.

1
Day One — Arrival & Village Immersion
Get to know Hobart at a leisurely pace
Afternoon

Arrive & Settle In

Check into the Inn or your rental. Walk Main Street end to end. Pop into a couple of shops that catch your eye.

Evening

Welcome Dinner at The Inn Bar

Craft cocktails, local fare, and conversation with fellow travelers and village regulars.

2
Day Two — The Grand Bookshop Tour
Every shop, every shelf, all day long
Morning

Coffee & Start at Blenheim Hill

Begin the full crawl. Take your time in each shop — talk to the owners, hear their stories.

Midday

Antiquarian & Liberty Rock

Lose yourself in three floors of pre-1850 treasures, then browse the village's largest collection.

Afternoon

Around the Corner & More

Creative Corner, NY Ephemera, Quarry Books, and More Good Books. Finish the set.

Evening

Organize Your Haul

Spread your finds on the bed, stack them up, take a photo. You've earned a quiet evening with a good book.

3
Day Three — The Catskills & Beyond
Mountains, rivers, and farm country
Morning

Hike the Catskills

Head to a nearby trail for a morning hike. Options range from gentle creek walks to mountain summit scrambles.

Midday

Explore Surrounding Towns

Visit Margaretville, Roxbury, or Stamford for antique shops, farm-to-table restaurants, and local color.

Afternoon

Return & Farewell

One last pass through your favorite bookshop, a goodbye drink at the bar, and head home with a car full of books.

💡

Pro tip: Three days is ideal for combining the book village with Catskill outdoor adventures. Plan around a weekend to catch all shops open.

1
Monday — Arrive & Decompress
Leave the city behind
Afternoon

Check In & Unwind

Settle into your cabin, Airbnb, or Inn room. Walk the village, breathe the mountain air, and let the pace of rural life take over.

Evening

Dinner & Early Rest

Simple dinner, early bed. You have a whole week — no rush.

2
Tuesday — River & Reading Day
Find your spot and read all day
Morning

West Branch of the Delaware

Walk or drive to the river. Find a rock, set up a chair, and read with the sound of water as your soundtrack.

Afternoon

Village Wandering

Explore Main Street at your own pace. Chat with locals. Discover what's around each corner.

3
Wednesday — Farm Country
Explore the agricultural roots of Delaware County
All Day

Farm Stands & Back Roads

Drive the back roads of Delaware County. Visit farm stands, pick up local cheese, maple syrup, and produce. Stop for lunch in Margaretville or Andes.

4
Thursday — Catskill Adventure Day
Mountains, swimming holes, and covered bridges
Morning

Hike a Catskill Peak

Take on a proper mountain hike. Balsam Lake, Bearpen, or Vly — all reachable from Hobart.

Afternoon

Swimming Holes & Waterfalls

Cool off at a local swimming hole or seek out one of the area's hidden waterfalls.

Evening

Drinks at The Inn Bar

You've earned a cold one. Share trail stories with fellow visitors at the bar.

5-6
Friday & Saturday — The Full Bookshop Experience
All eight shops, no rushing
Two Days

Spend Two Full Days in the Shops

With all shops open on Saturday, split your browsing across two days. Spend an hour in each store. Talk to the owners. Learn the stories behind the books and the buildings. This is what you came for.

7
Sunday — Slow Farewell
One last morning in the mountains
Morning

Final Walk & Favorite Shop

Walk Main Street one more time. Pop into your favorite shop to say goodbye and grab that last book.

Midday

Head Home

Pack the car with books, memories, and mountain air. You'll be back — everyone comes back.

💡

Pro tip: A full week lets you truly disconnect. Leave the laptop at home, bring a reading list, and let the village work its magic.
